Overview

QR codes are a convenient way to access websites, make payments, or store information using just a scan. However, there are dangers associated with their use, especially when scanned from untrusted sources.

Risks of Scanning QR Codes

  • Phishing: Scanning a malicious QR code may redirect you to a fake website designed to steal your personal information.
  • Malware: Some QR codes can trigger the download of malware or spyware onto your device, compromising your security.
  • Data Harvesting: QR codes may lead to websites that collect personal data without your knowledge or consent.
  • Unwanted Charges: Scanning a fraudulent QR code might unknowingly sign you up for paid services or subscriptions.

How to Avoid QR Code Threats

To reduce the risks associated with QR codes, consider these safety tips:

  • Only scan QR codes from trusted sources, such as well-known businesses or official communications.
  • Use a QR code scanner app that previews the link before opening it.
  • Avoid scanning codes on public or unverified materials, like posters or flyers.
  • Be cautious of codes that trigger automatic actions (like dialing a phone number or sending a message).
